MY EDUCATION

EDUCATION

From the Introduction can see that I was born and raised in Nigeria In the city of Lagos. I started school at the age of 3 at Saint Benedict. I cant quite remember much about the school but i will always remember it for its fun and all inclusive activities. I strictly remember a game of pick and count the beans, fumny times.

Going up to primary school I had amazing friends and teachers and We used to do these fun projects every week and I frankly remember the time i couldn't spell the word English and I used to spell it as Inglish. In primary school you have 6 years but i only did 5 and i skipped the sixth because in Nigeria it is relatively normal to skip grade 6 and I was like why not.

Highschool, Rather Secondary school as we call it In Nigeria was quite a roller coaster! I wasn't really popular or every the pretty girl. In my fist year of secondary school I was quite a gullible person, people always said i was too kind but thats the only life i ever really knew. Going to senior school I finally found myself and who my friend really were, Secondary school was basically a make or break situation. There are alot of people who pretend to be your friends but dont even like you, we call those (FAKE FRIENDS! )

This is a Picture of Amala and Ewedu

This is my favorite food, Amala and Ewedu, a traditional Nigerian dish. It consists of yam flour dough (Amala) served with a delicious green stew made from Jute leaves (Ewedu). It’s a favorite for many Nigerians and especially enjoyed with meat or fish.

Picture of the Buses in Lagos
